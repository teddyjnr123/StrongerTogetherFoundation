Project portfolio & methodology

Back-to-School Drive — supporting access to education

The Back-to-School Drive identifies children who are at risk of non-attendance due to lack of basic supplies. Working with school administrators and community committees, we deliver complete school kits containing uniforms, textbooks, exercise books and stationery. In addition to material support, the programme funds remedial sessions for learners who fall behind, and trains volunteer tutors to sustain progress after the distribution.

Monitoring: Attendance logs are compared termly; reading fluency checks are conducted at the start and end of every term to quantify learning gains.

Healthy Girls Programme — menstrual health and dignity

Menstrual health is central to girls’ ability to remain in school. Our Healthy Girls Programme provides reusable sanitary kits, private disposal solutions and structured health education sessions. The programme also supports school infrastructure upgrades where needed, including separate changing areas and improved sanitation facilities.

Monitoring: The programme tracks girls’ absence due to menstrual-related reasons and surveys participants on confidence and school participation following sessions.

Community Play Spaces — psychosocial and social development

Play is essential for cognitive and social-emotional development. We rehabilitate public spaces, provide safe equipment, and organise after-school clubs focused on mentorship, reading and life skills. Youth volunteers and local teachers run structured activities that improve school readiness and promote a sense of belonging.

Monitoring: Participation rates, teacher reports, and caregiver feedback form part of our evaluative framework.

How projects are selected & sustained

Project selection is guided by need assessments, stakeholder consultations and the potential for scalable outcomes. We prioritize projects that:

  • Address immediate barriers to education and health;
  • Leverage local leadership for implementation;
  • Include clear indicators and a sustainability plan.

Sustainability is achieved through community ownership, volunteer training and regular handover milestones. Our goal is to create models that local institutions can adopt and sustain with minimal external input.
